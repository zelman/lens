# Competitive Landscape

**Last updated:** 2026-03-23
**Entry count:** 5

## Executive Summary

The Lens Project occupies a unique position: deep identity discovery combined with structured output that powers automated matching. Direct competitors (Jack & Jill) have funding but lack depth, portability, and coach integration. Adjacent products solve pieces of the problem (resume formatting, personality assessment, job boards) but none combine discovery + structure + matching + human expertise in a single system. Our moat is the bidirectional lens concept—portable identity documents that work for both candidates and companies.

---

## Tier 1: Direct Competitors

Products attempting to solve identity-based job matching.

---

### Jack & Jill
**URL:** https://jackandjill.co
**Founded:** ~2023
**Funding:** $20M seed
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-23

**What they do:**
AI-powered career coaching platform. Conversational intake to understand career goals, then surfaces opportunities. Marketing emphasizes "knowing you" before matching.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
Direct overlap on identity-driven matching. Both premise that understanding the person deeply leads to better matches than keyword scanning.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Well-funded, likely larger team
- Consumer brand marketing
- Mobile-first approach (reported)

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Discovery depth: Multi-session structured intake vs. single conversation
- Portable output: User owns their lens document, can take it anywhere
- Coach network: Human expertise validates and refines AI output
- Bidirectional: Company-side lens enables true two-way matching
- Passive monitoring: Ongoing scoring vs. point-in-time matching

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: shallow vs. Lens **deep**
- Portable output: **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Scoring/matching: yes vs. Lens **yes**
- Coach integration: **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Bidirectional: **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Passive monitoring: unknown vs. Lens **yes**

**Verdict:**
Main threat due to funding, but fundamentally different depth model. They're optimizing for speed-to-match; we're optimizing for match quality. If they pivot to depth, competition intensifies.

## Tier 2: Adjacent Products

Tools that overlap on one dimension but don't solve the full problem.

---

### JSON Resume
**URL:** https://jsonresume.org
**Founded:** 2014
**Funding:** Open source / unfunded
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-23

**What they do:**
Open standard for structured resume data. JSON schema that various tools can read/write. Community-maintained.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
Establishes precedent for structured career documents. Proves the concept of portable, machine-readable professional identity.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Open standard, wide adoption
- Simple, well-documented schema
- Large ecosystem of themes/tools

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Not just resume facts—captures identity, values, energy
- Intake process that generates the document (not manual entry)
- Scoring system that acts on the structure
- Living document that evolves, not static snapshot

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: **none** (manual entry) vs. Lens deep
- Portable output: **yes** vs. Lens yes
- Scoring/matching: **no** vs. Lens yes
- Coach integration: **no** vs. Lens yes
- Bidirectional: **no** vs. Lens yes

**Verdict:**
Not a competitor—a precedent. Could potentially adopt or extend their schema for resume-facts portion of lens.

### DISC / StrengthsFinder / Myers-Briggs
**URL:** Various
**Founded:** 1928 / 2001 / 1962
**Funding:** Established enterprises
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-23

**What they do:**
Personality and strengths assessments. Produce reports classifying individuals into types or identifying top strengths/traits.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
Both try to capture "who someone is" in structured form. Often used in hiring and team composition.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Decades of research and validation
- Brand recognition, enterprise acceptance
- Certified practitioner networks

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Career-specific, not general personality
- Forward-matching to opportunities, not just self-understanding
- Evolving document, not point-in-time assessment
- Captures context (work style, energy, sector fit) not just traits

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: moderate (questionnaire) vs. Lens deep (conversation)
- Portable output: **PDF report** vs. Lens **structured + narrative**
- Scoring/matching: **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Coach integration: sometimes vs. Lens **built-in**
- Bidirectional: **no** vs. Lens yes

**Verdict:**
Different category. These are inputs to self-knowledge; we're outputs to action. Could integrate assessment results as lens inputs.

### 360 Feedback Tools (Culture Amp, Lattice, 15Five)
**URL:** Various
**Founded:** 2010s
**Funding:** Well-funded (Culture Amp $260M+)
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-23

**What they do:**
Collect multi-rater feedback on employees. Performance management, engagement surveys, development tracking.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
Capture "who someone is" through external observations. Used for development and sometimes hiring.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Multi-perspective (not just self-report)
- Integrated with performance management
- Enterprise-grade, compliance-ready

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Forward-looking (where should you go?) vs. backward-looking (how did you do?)
- Self-directed, candidate-owned vs. employer-owned
- Job matching, not performance review
- Works for job seekers, not just employed people

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: moderate (survey-based) vs. Lens deep
- Portable output: **no** (employer-owned) vs. Lens **yes**
- Scoring/matching: **no** vs. Lens yes
- Bidirectional: **no** vs. Lens yes

**Verdict:**
Different use case. They own the "how am I doing" space; we own the "where should I go" space. Potential integration for employed users.

## Tier 3: Ecosystem Players

Platforms that shape the market or could integrate with Lens.

---

### ATS Systems (Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby)
**URL:** greenhouse.io, lever.co, ashbyhq.com
**Founded:** 2012 / 2012 / 2018
**Funding:** Greenhouse $500M+, Lever $73M, Ashby $30M
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-23

**What they do:**
Applicant Tracking Systems. Manage hiring pipeline, parse resumes, enable collaboration on candidates. Keyword matching, structured hiring processes.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
Both involved in matching candidates to jobs. ATS is employer-side infrastructure.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Entrenched in enterprise workflows
- Full hiring pipeline management
- Compliance, reporting, integrations

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Candidate-side, not employer-side
- Identity-based matching vs. keyword scanning
- Candidate owns their data
- Fundamentally different matching philosophy (understand the person vs. parse the resume)

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: **none** (resume parsing) vs. Lens deep
- Portable output: N/A (employer tool)
- Scoring/matching: keyword-based vs. Lens identity-based
- Bidirectional: one-way (employer→candidate)

**Verdict:**
Not competitors—opposite side of the market. Potential integration partners. Company-side lens could feed into ATS for better matching.

---

### LinkedIn / Indeed
**URL:** linkedin.com, indeed.com
**Founded:** 2003 / 2004
**Funding:** Public companies
**Last reviewed:** 2026-03-23

**What they do:**
Job boards and professional networking. Aggregation, search, apply flows. LinkedIn adds networking and content.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
Massive reach. Where most job searching happens. Profile = identity representation.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Scale, network effects
- Employer advertising revenue
- Data on who's hiring, who's looking

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Depth over breadth
- User-owned identity document
- Quality over volume matching
- Coach network for human validation

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: **shallow** (profile fields) vs. Lens deep
- Portable output: **no** (platform-locked) vs. Lens yes
- Scoring/matching: algorithmic (opaque) vs. Lens transparent
- Coach integration: **no** vs. Lens yes

**Verdict:**
Too big to compete with directly. We're not replacing LinkedIn—we're adding a depth layer. Lens insights could inform how users present on LinkedIn.
