## Executive Summary

No product in the market runs coaching-depth discovery on both sides of a hire (role AND candidate) and produces a portable narrative artifact the candidate owns. That remains the Lens wedge.

The landscape breaks into three clusters worth understanding distinctly:

1. **Conversational AI matching (Tier 1, emerging):** Jack & Jill ($20M seed, Oct 2025) is the nearest competitor in thesis — they have also concluded that "the information needed to make a good match is conversational, not documentary." They operate at volume, voice-first, for general hiring. They do not target retained exec search, do not produce a candidate-owned artifact, and have no coach layer. Closest to us on thesis, furthest from us on buyer and format.

2. **Assessment incumbents (Tier 2, established):** The Predictive Index is the canonical "assessment verdict" product — $102M revenue, 70-year validity moat, deeply embedded in retained search via Caldwell Partners (PI's largest certified partner worldwide). PI Job Assessment is the closest functional analog to Role Lens. Lens's positioning against PI: *"PI gives you a graph; Lens gives you a conversation."* Do NOT compete on validity, speed, or platform breadth.

3. **Adjacent and ecosystem (Tier 2 + 3):** SquarePeg pivoted away from bidirectional consumer matching toward B2B sourcing/screening — informative prior art on what fails. Pymetrics was acquired by Harver (2022); consolidation signal. DISC / MBTI / StrengthsFinder are static traits with no matching layer. LinkedIn / Indeed / Greenhouse / Lever shape distribution and integration expectations, not direct competition.

**GTM implication:** retained search channel is already penetrated by PI. Assume PI is present in every mid-to-large retained firm conversation. Wedge strategy: (a) target firms not yet PI-certified; (b) position as complement to PI, not replacement; (c) lead with the candidate-owned narrative artifact, which PI structurally cannot match. Accelerating the coach-persona channel (James Pratt and successors) is our structural answer to PI's certified partner network.

## Tier 1: Direct Competitors

*Products attempting to solve the same core problem: identity-based matching via depth of discovery rather than keyword matching or static traits.*

---

### Jack & Jill
**URL:** jackandjill.ai (London-based; also marketed as jackandjill.com)
**Founded:** Early 2025
**Funding:** $20M seed led by Creandum (Oct 2025). Other investors: Dig Ventures, Entrepreneurs First, Ada Ventures, Firedrop, Repeat.vc, Episode 1, Playfair, and 75+ angels including Nico Rosberg and employees of Lovable, Anthropic, and ElevenLabs.
**Founders:** Matthew Wilson (previously founded Omnipresent), Saaras Mehan (YC-backed founder of Kular.ai).
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

**What they do:**
Two autonomous conversational AI agents: "Jack" interviews candidates via phone conversation to build a rich profile of ambitions, working style, and skills that goes beyond the CV; "Jill" works with hiring managers to understand role requirements, then scans Jack's candidate network to surface a small number of well-matched candidates per role. Pitch: "the service of a world-class recruiter at a fraction of the cost." Claims 60% faster recruitment, 2x candidate engagement. 49,000 candidates had spoken with Jack by Oct 2025 (six months in); hundreds of London-based companies using Jill (e.g., TrueLayer). Expanding to San Francisco.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
- **Shared core thesis:** Jack & Jill has publicly concluded that conversation beats documents for match quality. Their founder-facing statement — "the information needed to make a good match is conversational, not documentary" — is essentially our wedge statement. This is the closest thesis match in the landscape.
- **Conversational intake on candidate side** (Jack) and role-requirements conversation on employer side (Jill) mirrors our Candidate Lens + Role Lens bidirectional structure.
- **Structured profile beyond CV** — they explicitly frame the CV as insufficient, same premise as ours.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- **Volume economics:** autonomous AI agents handle thousands of simultaneous conversations. Replacing agency recruiters at volume, not enhancing retained searches at the top end.
- **Voice-first interface:** "It all starts with a phone call." Phone is lower friction than a 45-minute written session.
- **Network effect:** Jill pulls from Jack's proprietary candidate pool. Every candidate Jack onboards makes Jill's match surface better. Classic two-sided marketplace flywheel.
- **Capital, team, momentum:** $20M seed, tier-one investors, two serial founders, 6-month traction story.
- **Geography:** London-first, SF next — already operating at a scale we are not.

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- **Coaching depth vs. conversational breadth:** their phone call is minutes of structured interview. Our session is 45 minutes of coaching-methodology-driven discovery (James Pratt persona encoded; future coach personas). Different quality of signal.
- **Portable candidate artifact:** Jack's profile lives inside Jack & Jill's system as input to Jill's matching. Candidate does not receive a document they can carry elsewhere. Lens produces a markdown + YAML the candidate owns outright.
- **Coach layer:** no coach persona model. Jack is a single AI agent applying Jack & Jill's own methodology.
- **Buyer focus:** Jack & Jill pursues volume hiring (fast-growing companies, general roles). Lens targets $300K+ retained executive search, where mis-hire cost is catastrophic and per-search price supports deep discovery.
- **Output format:** Jack & Jill produces a matching service (AI-to-AI); Lens produces a shared narrative artifact that powers recruiter↔candidate, recruiter↔client, peer↔peer conversations.

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: Jack & Jill **moderate** (structured phone interview, minutes) vs. Lens **deep** (coaching-methodology session, 45 min)
- Portable output: Jack & Jill **no** (profile internal to their matching system) vs. Lens **yes**
- Scoring/matching: Jack & Jill **yes** (Jill ↔ Jack) vs. Lens **yes** (Role Lens ↔ Candidate Lens)
- Coach integration: Jack & Jill **no** (single AI methodology) vs. Lens **yes** (persona model)
- Bidirectional: Jack & Jill **yes** vs. Lens **yes**
- Passive monitoring: Jack & Jill **unknown/likely yes** (network persists) vs. Lens **planned** (pipeline briefings)
- Conversation catalyst artifact: Jack & Jill **no** (output is a match) vs. Lens **yes** (output is a document)
- Candidate agency: Jack & Jill **low** (profile bound to their platform) vs. Lens **high** (candidate-owned)

**Verdict:**
Closest thesis match in the landscape. Capital, momentum, and founder caliber mean they will scale — but they're building volume consumer/SMB hiring, not retained executive search, and their network-effect moat requires keeping the candidate profile locked to their platform (structurally opposite to our portable-artifact wedge). We should assume Jack & Jill becomes the dominant player in general AI-conversational hiring within 18 months. Our defensible position is retained exec search ($300K+) + portable candidate-owned narrative + coach persona layer, which are all things Jack & Jill has explicitly not built and would contradict their unit economics to build. **Watch closely; don't mimic.** If they announce an exec-search tier or a candidate-owned output, threat level escalates immediately.

### SquarePeg
**URL:** squarepeg.ai (formerly squarepeghires.com)
**Founded:** ~2015 (New York, NY)
**Funding:** Not disclosed publicly at scale; early-stage company.
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

**What they do:**
Originally launched as a two-sided consumer-facing job matching platform using "JobFit" assessments across 19 personality traits, matching candidates to jobs based on fit beyond keywords. Pivoted to an AI-assisted B2B recruiting platform for tech companies — sourcing, screening, scoring, and ranking applicants against a job description, with ATS integrations (Lever, Greenhouse). Pitches itself as an agency-recruiter replacement for lean teams: "set-it-and-forget-it."

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
- **Original thesis directly overlapped:** bidirectional fit-based matching beyond keywords was SquarePeg's founding pitch. This is the closest prior-art for our exact wedge.
- **Job-description-to-candidate-profile scoring** persists in current product.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- **ATS integration depth** — core part of their current value prop.
- **Continuous passive sourcing** driven by job requirements.
- **Established B2B distribution** via Lever Marketplace and similar channels.

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- **Still pursuing the bidirectional consumer-facing thesis they abandoned.** Their pivot is evidence that pure consumer-facing bidirectional matching is hard to build a business around; enterprise sourcing is easier to sell.
- **Candidate-owned artifact** — SquarePeg keeps profiles in its system; candidates don't own their fit data.
- **Coaching depth** — they use trait assessments, not coaching-methodology conversations.
- **Retained exec search focus** — SquarePeg targets lean tech teams, not $300K+ exec searches.

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: SquarePeg **shallow-moderate** (trait assessment + ATS job description) vs. Lens **deep**
- Portable output: SquarePeg **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Scoring/matching: SquarePeg **yes** (trait match score) vs. Lens **yes**
- Coach integration: SquarePeg **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Bidirectional: SquarePeg **no longer** (was yes at launch; pivoted to B2B one-sided) vs. Lens **yes**
- Passive monitoring: SquarePeg **yes** (continuous sourcing) vs. Lens **planned**

**Verdict:**
The critical prior-art lesson: bidirectional consumer-facing matching is hard. SquarePeg started where we are and moved away from it under commercial pressure. Two non-obvious takeaways: (1) the business gravity pulls toward employer-paid B2B sourcing, not candidate-side portable artifacts — we need to actively resist that pull or embrace it on our terms (retained exec search is employer-paid but with a candidate-owned artifact); (2) trait-based matching without coaching depth is a commodity features set that gets consumed by larger platforms. Our depth + narrative + portability triad is what SquarePeg didn't have. Threat level: Low (they don't target our segment). Strategic value: High (read their pivot carefully before committing to pricing and buyer).

### The Predictive Index (PI)
**URL:** predictiveindex.com
**Founded:** 1955 (HQ Westwood, MA)
**Funding:** $101M total across 4 rounds. Latest: $30M from JM Family Enterprises, Sep 2022. Earlier lead investors include General Catalyst and Escalate Capital Partners. Per Tracxn, ownership now sits with Phoenix Strategy Investments (CEO Mike Zani's search fund) — effectively founder-controlled.
**Scale:** ~447 employees (21 quota-carrying sales reps, 34 engineers, 15 marketers); 8,000 customers across 142 countries; $101.9M revenue (2024, per Latka); 65+ years of operation; 383+ validity studies; 10,000+ organizations historically; integrates with 97+ HR platforms (Greenhouse, Workday, ADP, Paylocity, BambooHR).
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

**What they do:**
Talent optimization platform built around a 6-minute free-choice adjective checklist ("PI Behavioral Assessment") and a cognitive assessment. Produces a 4-factor behavioral profile (Dominance, Extraversion, Patience, Formality), a reference-profile label, and match scores against job targets. Wraps the core science in workshops, consulting, a certified partner network, and post-hire modules (PI Perform, engagement surveys, team dynamics, leadership development).

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
- **PI Job Assessment (JA)** is the closest functional analog to Role Lens that exists in mass market. Multi-stakeholder input via emailed questionnaire, consensus-building on behavioral/cognitive requirements, option to benchmark against internal role models, guided target-setting process. Output: a job target that candidate assessments are scored against.
- **Bidirectional scoring model** — candidate profile ↔ job target match score is their core hiring workflow.
- **Retained search channel penetration** — Caldwell Partners announced itself as PI's largest certified partner in the world in 2019. Boutique retained firms (Renée Vincent, Shulman Associates, Ascent Select) are also in the certified partner network. This is the exact buyer segment we're targeting.
- **Post-hire extension** — PI Perform handles 1:1s, performance conversations, team dynamics. They own ground we haven't planned to enter.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- **Validity moat:** 383+ validity studies over 70 years, EEOC / Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures alignment, SOC 2 Type II, ISO/IEC 27001:2022. Legal-defensibility story we cannot match.
- **Speed of intake:** 6 minutes vs. our 45-minute coaching-depth session. Zero candidate friction.
- **Established channel:** certified partner network of coaches, consultants, and search firms already trained and selling PI into our target segment.
- **Platform breadth:** post-hire use cases (performance, engagement, leadership dev) extend contract far beyond a single search.
- **Integration footprint:** native Greenhouse / Workday / ADP / Paylocity hooks.
- **Brand:** 70-year-old name. CEO Mike Zani's book and podcast (*The Science of Dream Teams*) reinforce the category narrative.

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- **Depth of discovery:** coaching-depth conversation vs. forced-choice adjective checklist. PI captures traits; Lens captures narrative, tensions, values, disqualifiers, mission, situation.
- **Portable output:** candidate owns a markdown document (+ YAML) they can carry to any search, any firm, any coach. PI profiles live in the customer's PI tenant.
- **Conversation catalyst, not verdict:** PI outputs a 4-factor graph and a reference-profile label. Lens outputs a narrative document designed to enable recruiter↔candidate, recruiter↔client, and peer↔peer conversations. PI is the archetype of the "assessment verdict" model we have explicitly positioned against.
- **Coach-in-the-loop:** James Pratt persona and future coach personas encode real coaching methodology into discovery. PI's partner network are users of the tool, not contributors to the intelligence model.
- **Narrative + signal:** markdown + YAML gives both human readability and machine scoring.
- **Forward-looking match hypothesis** (in progress) vs. PI's backward-looking trait profile.
- **Candidate-side product:** Lens starts with the candidate and extends to the role. PI is company-side only.

**Gap analysis:**
- Discovery depth: PI **shallow** (6-min checklist) vs. Lens **deep**
- Portable output: PI **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Scoring/matching: PI **yes** vs. Lens **yes**
- Coach integration: PI **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Bidirectional: PI **yes** vs. Lens **yes**
- Passive monitoring: PI **partial** (post-hire) vs. Lens **planned**
- Narrative artifact: PI **no** vs. Lens **yes**
- Candidate agency: PI **none** vs. Lens **high**

**Verdict:**
Big, credentialed, and embedded in the retained search channel — the incumbent example of the "assessment verdict" model we have explicitly rejected. PI validates the buyer (Caldwell alone is proof of concept) but locks in that buyer with a 70-year validity story and integration footprint impossible to out-build. Our wedge is NOT "better assessment"; it's depth + portable candidate artifact + conversation catalyst. When a prospect says "we already use PI," the answer is: *"Great — Lens fits alongside PI. PI gives you a behavioral graph; Lens gives the candidate a narrative the graph can't produce, and gives the recruiter a shared artifact to use in the client meeting."* Do NOT compete on validity, speed, or platform breadth. DO compete on depth, narrative, ownership, and the catalyst framing.

### Torre.ai
**URL:** torre.ai (Torre Labs)
**Founded:** 2017
**Funding:** ~$4M disclosed.
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21 — bootstrap, re-verify

**What they do:**
Candidate-profile-first job platform. Candidates build rich structured profiles ("Genomes") with strengths, experiences, and preferences; employers post "Opportunities" and Torre matches. Bi-directional by design. Open-source ethos; emphasizes portable profiles.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
- **Candidate-owned rich profile** is Torre's founding premise — closer to Lens on portability than most competitors.
- Bi-directional matching.
- Signal-over-keyword framing.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Portable "Genome" structured profile — the closest public analog to our candidate-owned markdown artifact.
- Open-by-default, public-facing profiles (closer to LinkedIn than to a recruitment tool).
- Global candidate base; remote-work positioning.

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Coaching-depth discovery vs. self-populated form.
- Narrative + YAML (not just structured fields).
- Role Lens on employer side — Torre's job postings are conventional.
- Retained exec search focus; Torre is horizontal, skewed toward remote tech.

**Verdict:**
The most ideologically aligned player in the landscape on **portability** — candidates actually own their Torre Genome. But Torre operates at the profile-form layer (self-fill) rather than coaching-discovery layer, and targets a different buyer (remote tech jobs, horizontal). Threat level: Low. Strategic value: High as a format reference — study how they structure portability, public/private lens toggles, and profile versioning before finalizing our schema.

### DISC / StrengthsFinder / MBTI (category entry)
**URL:** multiple (Everything DiSC, Gallup StrengthsFinder, Myers-Briggs Foundation, 16personalities, etc.)
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

**What they do:**
Category of static self-report personality/strengths assessments with no matching layer. Output is a type code (INTJ, Activator, "D-style") or a ranked strengths list. Sold into corporate training, coaching, team development. Not hiring tools in the primary use case, but often cited alongside PI in talent-optimization conversations.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
- Self-report discovery of preferences and styles.
- Used in coaching contexts (James Pratt's methodology touches on similar territory).

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Vast installed base; assumed literacy among HR and coaching professionals.
- Cheap, fast, widely understood.

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Narrative vs. type code.
- Matching layer (no DISC/MBTI product natively matches candidates to roles with a score).
- Coaching depth (coach interprets MBTI; Lens *is* the coaching conversation encoded).

**Verdict:**
These are **background noise in the category**, not direct competitors. When a recruiter or coach says "we use DISC," we're not replacing DISC — we're offering the next layer. Use them as comparison points for "Lens is not a personality type" positioning. Threat level: Low.

### 360 Feedback tools (category entry)
**URL:** multiple (Culture Amp, Lattice, Leapsome, Reflektive, CEB/SHL, dedicated 360 providers)
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

**What they do:**
Multi-rater feedback collection from peers, reports, and managers. Used post-hire for performance reviews, leadership development, coaching context. Backward-looking snapshot of how a person has shown up.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
- Closest mechanism to the Oh/Wang/Mount (2011) observer-ratings ceiling we reference in the Lens narrative.
- Potential future data source if Lens extends to observer-augmented profiles.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Multi-rater data (closer to psychometric ceiling than any self-report tool).
- Established post-hire workflow.

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Forward-looking match, not backward-looking review.
- Structured narrative, not rater ratings.
- Candidate-owned.

**Verdict:**
Adjacent, not competitive. Potential future integration surface ("attach 360 data to your lens for deeper signal"). Threat level: Low. Research the best-in-class format before designing any Lens extension that incorporates observer input — we should not reinvent 360.

### Coaching platforms (BetterUp, Torch, CoachHub — category entry)
**URL:** betterup.com, torch.io, coachhub.com
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21 — bootstrap, re-verify

Enterprise coaching-at-scale platforms. Match employees to human coaches, facilitate session scheduling, track goals and outcomes, report to HR. BetterUp is the scale leader (~$4.7B valuation at peak).

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Human coach network at scale.

**Key differentiators (ours):**

**Verdict:**
These are **partners and referral sources**, not competitors — at least until one of them productizes coaching into an AI persona, at which point we'd be in a different conversation. BetterUp in particular has the capital and position to launch a Jack-&-Jill-style conversational product if they chose to. Threat level: Low-Medium. Flag for monitoring if any of the big three announces generative-AI-driven coaching products.

### JSON Resume
**URL:** jsonresume.org
**Founded:** 2014 (open-source community project)
**Funding:** None (open source)
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

**What they do:**
Open-source structured JSON schema for resumes. Themable, versionable, portable. Not a product; a format standard with a growing community of tooling.

**Overlap with Lens Project:**
- **Format precedent** for candidate-owned, portable, structured career artifacts. Our markdown+YAML lens schema sits in the same design tradition.
- Reminds us what "portable" can look like when a community adopts a schema.

**Key differentiators (theirs):**
- Open-source, zero-vendor. Portability guaranteed by design.
- Installed tooling (CLI, themes, converters).

**Key differentiators (ours):**
- Narrative + structured signal, not just structured experience history.
- Coaching-discovery provenance (a JSON Resume is self-filled; a Lens is discovered).
- Match-scoring layer.

**Verdict:**
**Not a competitor; a format reference.** Long-term, Lens could/should publish an open schema that interoperates with JSON Resume (resume as input; lens as distinct downstream artifact). This connects to the PKI / protocol thesis in the Lens long-term vision. Threat level: None. Strategic value: High on format interoperability planning.

## Tier 3: Ecosystem Players

*Platforms that shape the market and integration expectations. We don't compete with them; we navigate around or through them.*

---

### LinkedIn
**URL:** linkedin.com, business.linkedin.com/talent-solutions
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-21

**Role in the ecosystem:**
Default professional profile layer globally; LinkedIn Recruiter is the dominant sourcing tool for retained and contingent search alike. LinkedIn Talent Insights and Talent Hub provide enterprise-grade workflow. ~1B members worldwide.

**Relevance to Lens:**
- **Every candidate in our target market already has a LinkedIn profile.** Lens must ingest LinkedIn data as an input, not duplicate it.
- LinkedIn profiles are LinkedIn-owned; our lens is candidate-owned. This is a structural difference to lead with.
- LinkedIn's "Open to Work" signal + skills endorsements are competing weakly with the signal-extraction layer we produce.

**Strategic posture:** integrate (import from LinkedIn), don't compete. Long-term: position Lens as "your LinkedIn profile describes where you've been; your lens describes who you are and where you're heading."
