## Why this document exists

Jack & Jill ($20M seed, Oct 2025, Creandum-led) has publicly stated the same thesis as Lens: *"the information needed to make a good match is conversational, not documentary."* Their founder pitch is our wedge statement. We cannot distinguish by saying "we believe in deep discovery and they don't" — they do too. We need a sharper frame.

The old framing was: *they're a competitor but they lack depth / portable document / coaches.* The "no depth" part is wrong — Jack does conversational discovery. The "no portable document / no coaches" parts are right but need to be articulated as a strategic choice, not a gap in their product. They chose not to build those things because their business model contradicts them.

The new framing has **four axes of distinction**, and each is defensible on structural grounds.

---

## The four axes

### 1. Buyer

**Jack & Jill:** volume hiring for fast-growing companies. 49,000 candidates onboarded in 6 months; hundreds of London companies using Jill. Unit economics work at SMB / mid-market scale with many roles per account.

**Lens:** retained executive search, $300K+ placements. Low volume, high per-search value. Mis-hire cost is catastrophic; recruiter fees are $100K+ per search. Buyer is the firm, not the hiring company.

**Why this is structural, not a pricing choice:** Jack & Jill's economics require thousands of simultaneous conversations per day. Retained exec search requires tens of deeply-considered searches per year per firm. You cannot be both products at once — they optimize for inverse metrics.

**Talking point:** *"Jack & Jill is building the software version of an agency recruiter — volume, speed, lower cost per placement. We're building the software version of an executive search partner — depth, rigor, higher trust per search. Different products for different problems."*

---

### 2. Output

**Jack & Jill:** the product is a **match service**. Jack talks to candidates, Jill talks to companies, and Jill surfaces a short list. The candidate never sees a document summarizing what Jack learned. The company never sees a document about a candidate — they see matches with context.

**Lens:** the product is a **document**. The candidate owns it. The recruiter uses it in the client meeting. The client reads it before the interview. Conversations happen *around* the document.

**Why this is structural:** Jack & Jill's value prop is "fewer interviews, faster placements" — that requires the match service to be the terminal product. Once you give candidates a document they own, the service becomes a document-generator with matching as a downstream feature. Different product shape.

**Talking point:** *"Jack & Jill produces a match. Lens produces an artifact. The artifact is what lets a candidate, a recruiter, and a client have a grounded conversation. The match is what ends one."*

---

### 3. Value proposition to the buyer

**Jack & Jill:** *"Replace your recruiter. Hire faster, cheaper."* They're selling labor cost reduction. Half the cost of traditional recruiting, per their pitch.

**Lens:** *"Make your recruiter more trusted. Win more searches. Prevent the expensive mis-hire."* We're selling decision quality and competitive differentiation to firms whose revenue depends on getting placements right, not on doing them cheaper.

**Why this is structural:** A retained search firm charging 33% of first-year comp does not want a tool that undercuts their labor premium. They want a tool that makes their rigor visible to clients and helps them win against other firms. Jack & Jill is a threat to retained firm economics; Lens is a lever for them.

**Talking point:** *"Every dollar Jack & Jill saves a hiring company comes out of a recruiter's fee. Every dollar Lens saves comes out of the cost of the wrong hire. Retained firms are our allies; they are Jack & Jill's disruption target."*

---

### 4. Moat

**Jack & Jill:** network effect. Jill gets better as Jack's candidate pool grows. Classic two-sided marketplace. The moat depends on keeping the candidate profile locked to their platform — if Jack's conversations became portable, the flywheel breaks.

**Lens:** format + coach partnership. The Lens document is portable by design (candidate owns the .md); the moat is (a) recruiter adoption of the format and (b) the coach persona network that powers discovery. A competitor can copy the format — that's the point, we want them to. What they cannot copy is an individual executive coach's methodology encoded as a persona, the credibility of the search-industry advisors who stand behind the product, or the per-firm customizations that live on top of the open format.

**Why this is structural:** Jack & Jill must keep the candidate locked in; we must keep the candidate free. These are incompatible moats. If either of us succeeds, we cannot also become the other.

**Talking point:** *"Jack & Jill's moat is a network that keeps candidates in their platform. Ours is a format that candidates carry out. If they succeed, candidates are stuck. If we succeed, candidates are portable. You're betting on one direction of the arrow."*

---

## What Jack & Jill does that we should respect

Honest read — they're not weak:

- **$20M seed from Creandum + Anthropic/Lovable/ElevenLabs angels is serious capital.** Us explaining our position cannot wave them off.
- **Matthew Wilson (Omnipresent) and Saaras Mehan (Kular.ai, YC) are repeat founders.** They will execute.
- **49K candidates in 6 months is real traction.** Not vaporware.
- **"It all starts with a phone call" is a better intake UX than our 45-min written session for candidates who don't yet know they want deep discovery.** Lower friction.
- **They have a better story for LPs** (agent-AI, volume, network effect) than we do today (narrow buyer, depth over speed).

When pitching, don't pretend Jack & Jill doesn't exist or doesn't matter. Acknowledge them, then position.

## Audience-specific positioning

### For a VC meeting

*"Jack & Jill is building the mass-market AI recruiter. We're building the operating layer for retained executive search — a $20B+ global market with different economics. The founders of Jack & Jill and the founders of Lens are solving adjacent problems with structurally incompatible business models. The question for you is whether retained exec search gets its own AI-native infrastructure play, or gets absorbed into a volume platform. We think it gets its own, and here's why."*

### For a retained search firm leader

*"You've probably already heard about Jack & Jill. Their pitch is that AI agents will replace recruiters. Ours is the opposite: AI tools will make trusted recruiters more visible to clients and help you win more searches. Jack & Jill is your disruption threat. Lens is a tool in your hand. We'd like to show you how it works."*

### For a prospective executive coach partner

*"Jack & Jill treats the discovery conversation as a commodity — one AI does it for everyone. We treat it as your methodology, encoded as a persona. If you believe your coaching IP is valuable, we're the partner who says so. Jack & Jill is the partner who says it doesn't matter who runs the conversation."*

### For a candidate tester

*"Jack & Jill wants to match you faster to jobs. Lens wants to give you a document that describes you well enough for any recruiter, any search, any firm — for the rest of your career. Different products. If you want to end a job search quickly, use them. If you want to understand yourself well enough that any job search after this one is easier, use this."*

### For an advisor

*"The nearest competitor is Jack & Jill. They raised $20M in October to do conversational AI recruiting at volume. Same thesis as us on 'conversation beats resume,' opposite strategy on every other dimension: their buyer is volume hiring, their output is a match, their value is cost reduction, their moat is platform lock-in. Ours is the inverse on all four. The question I want your help with: is the retained exec search flank defensible against volume scaling down-market, or do we need to move faster?"*

## Three claims to drop from our pitch

1. *"Nobody is running deep discovery conversations on both sides of a hire."* Jack & Jill does (lighter-weight, but they do). Replace with: *"Nobody is producing a portable candidate-owned narrative document from that discovery."*

2. *"There's no depth in the market."* They have depth, just different depth. Replace with: *"There's no coaching-methodology depth in the market — no one else encodes a real executive coach's framework into their discovery."*

3. *"Jack & Jill lacks a coach network."* True but sounds dismissive. Replace with: *"Jack & Jill uses a single AI methodology applied uniformly. We build our discovery on top of individual executive coaching methodologies that become distinct personas."*

---

## Three claims to add

1. **Portability as first-principle differentiation.** Every pitch should include: *"The candidate owns their Lens. Jack & Jill's candidates do not own their profile. This is a deliberate strategic choice on both sides."*

2. **Retained-exec-search specificity.** Every pitch should name the buyer: *"Retained executive search firms placing $300K+ roles. Mis-hire cost is $1M+. Recruiter fees are $100K+. Not a volume market."*

3. **Coach persona as the unreplicable moat.** Every pitch should frame the coach persona model as a structural differentiator: *"Lens personas are built by encoding individual executive coaching methodologies — the kind of IP that takes a career to develop and is meaningful on both sides of a search conversation. Jack & Jill's single-AI-methodology approach cannot reach that depth without abandoning their unit economics."*

---

## What to monitor

Escalate threat level from Jack & Jill if any of the following happen:

1. They announce a tier for executive search or $200K+ roles.
2. They launch a candidate-facing profile view or portable export of Jack conversations.
3. They partner with named executive coaches or build a coach persona layer.
4. They raise a Series A on the back of US market traction.

Any one of these means the positioning axes above have narrowed and we need to re-differentiate. Calendar reminder: check their product announcements and LinkedIn monthly.
