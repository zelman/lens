## Essence

I build elegant structure from beautiful chaos, creating systems that can scale and bend without breaking. My craft is orchestrating aligned motion from early-stage energy — I architect 10% slack into everything because flexibility is essential and the unexpected is inevitable. I lead through contextual mix: directive when decisions need my judgment, facilitative when problems need their ownership, translational when direction comes from above. I show up with loyalty and seek consensus when possible, but I know when to call the play. I nurture both people and processes through the teachable moments embedded in growth, creating protected ecosystems where teams can flourish despite organizational chaos.

## Values

I create conditions where people can do their best work by absorbing the impact of poorly managed changes and leadership failures before they reach my team. I'm energized by the feedback loop of learning — their growth feeds my curiosity, their success validates the systems I've built. I need autonomy over hiring and firing decisions because micromanagement disrupts the careful conditions required for people to thrive. When leadership treats support teams as second-class citizens while celebrating revenue generators, I create recognition and stability through authentic gratitude and servant leadership, even without budget or organizational support. I work to make sure the thing being supported is actually worth supporting — I get drained maintaining infrastructure that serves hollow promises or broken products.

## Mission & Sector Fit

I'm drawn to building infrastructure for authentic self-knowledge that gets used, not just collected. The opportunity that most excites me is founding a company that creates "lens" technology — helping individuals assemble comprehensive archives of themselves to evaluate career fit, then scaling into organizational assessment tools that flip traditional hiring. I want to be the product architect and founder, building from scratch and selecting team members who will run with the vision. This sits at the intersection of AI/NLP, psychology, and product design in the emerging career/talent infrastructure sector. I'm energized by creating tools that help both people and organizations make better, more authentic matching decisions rather than performing for broken systems like ATS keyword optimization.

## Work Style

I work best in the building phase of organizations — the first 3-5 years where I can observe systems, diagnose what needs changing, and reconstruct rather than just maintain. My natural rhythm is heads-down morning intensity, afternoon step-back, and evening second wind, requiring protected sleep and local collaborators rather than global 24/7 chaos. I manage small direct teams while connecting broadly across the organization, using urgent collaboration that mines for productive conflict internally while staying empathetic and calm with customers. I assess people quickly through communication patterns — pace, tempo, word choice, inflection — and work flows most easily with others who can handle both confrontation and relationship preservation. I need latitude to envision better ways rather than execute prescribed processes, and naturally move on when roles shift from building to pure maintenance.

## What Fills Them

I'm energized by teaching and advising — seeing customers and coworkers succeed because of tools and guidance I've provided. I thrive when people actually want to use what I've built and when I can help them solve real problems with it. The building and systems work only energizes me when it supports genuine problem-solving and enables sincere teaching. I get drained maintaining infrastructure that serves hollow promises — when customers were sold something we're not, or when the product itself is broken. I need environments where the thing being supported is actually worth supporting, where customer enthusiasm is real rather than tolerant vendor management.
