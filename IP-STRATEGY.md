## What We Believe Is Novel

### 1. Bidirectional Lens Architecture
A system where both candidates AND companies/roles have structured identity documents ("lenses") that enable two-way matching. The candidate lens captures professional identity; the company/role lens captures culture, team dynamics, and role requirements. Matching scores both directions.

**Why novel:** Current systems are one-directional (candidate applies, employer filters). Two-way structured identity matching appears unaddressed in prior art.

### 2. Conversational Intake → Structured Output Pipeline
Multi-session AI-guided discovery that produces a machine-readable identity document with both structured data (YAML frontmatter) and narrative content (markdown body). The document is portable, user-owned, and versioned.

**Why novel:** Existing tools either do shallow intake (questionnaires) or produce unstructured output (coaching notes). The combination of deep conversational discovery + structured portable output appears novel.

### 3. Passive Opportunity Monitoring with Identity-Based Scoring
Continuous background evaluation of opportunities (jobs, companies, funding events) against a user's lens, producing scores and explanations. The user doesn't search; relevant opportunities surface automatically.

**Why novel:** Job alerts exist but use keyword matching. Scoring against a structured identity document with weighted dimensions and gates is different.

### 4. Coach-in-the-Loop Validation
Human coaches review and refine AI-generated lens documents before they're used for matching. The coach adds calibration that pure AI cannot.

**Why novel:** AI + human hybrid where the human validates identity capture (not just corrects errors) appears unaddressed.

## Prior Art Findings to Date

### Reviewed
- **JSON Resume** — Structured resume format, but no intake process, no matching, no identity beyond resume facts
- **DISC/StrengthsFinder/MBTI** — Personality assessment, but static, not career-specific, no matching
- **LinkedIn/Indeed** — Job matching, but shallow profiles, keyword-based, platform-locked
- **ATS systems** — Employer-side, resume parsing, no identity understanding
- **Jack & Jill** — Closest competitor; conversational but appears to lack depth, portability, bidirectionality

### Still to Review
- Academic literature on person-job fit
- HR tech patent landscape (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors)
- Career coaching software patents
- AI-assisted hiring tool patents

### Known Limitations
- "AI-assisted job matching" is a crowded space; claims must be specific
- "Structured document generation from conversation" may have prior art in other domains
- Bidirectionality is the strongest novel claim but needs validation

## Provisional Patent Scope (Draft)

**Working title:** System and Method for Bidirectional Identity-Based Career Matching

**Core claims (to validate):**
1. A method for generating structured professional identity documents through multi-session conversational AI intake
2. A system for matching candidates to opportunities using bidirectional identity documents
3. A method for continuous passive opportunity evaluation against a user's identity profile
4. A human-in-the-loop validation process for AI-generated identity documents

**Dependent claims to explore:**
- Specific structure of lens documents (YAML + markdown hybrid)
- Scoring algorithm with weighted dimensions and gates
- Coach network integration and certification
- Company-side lens generation process
