# Career Lens Document

## Essence

Systems-building leader who thrives in chaotic, fast-paced environments by creating order through process. Gathers input widely from all levels but moves quickly on decisions to maintain momentum. Has strong opinions and the drive to act on them — environments where problems are identified but can't be fixed are completely draining. Works best in reactive environments where frameworks can be built incrementally rather than getting trapped in perfectionism. ADHD means either overwhelmed by too many details or bored quickly, requiring dynamic work with visible impact and quick feedback loops.

## Values

Must feel like a builder with real ownership, not just an employee executing someone else's checklist. Requires structured approaches to conflict and decision-making — reactive chaos creates anxiety while thoughtful processes that embrace constructive disagreement enable best work. Needs ability to advocate for people as humans (both customers and team members), with honesty flowing upward. When candor becomes politically expensive or leadership treats feedback as negativity rather than useful information, cannot build anything real. Values transparency in compensation discussions, accountability in discussing departures, and psychological safety in team settings.

## Mission & Sector Fit

VC-backed Series A to early B companies (30-100 employees, under $75M raised) solving real problems for people who can't wait for better solutions. Drawn to B2B SaaS serving non-technical business users in healthcare operations, compliance-heavy environments (government/social services/legal/financial services), or workforce enablement. Thrives in the post-product-market-fit, pre-established-CS-function window where there's a blank canvas to build customer success under genuine growth pressure. Best work happens serving users who didn't choose technology but have to use it — clinic administrators, caseworkers, field technicians who measure success by whether Tuesday sucked less than Monday.

## Work Style

Remote work required with flexible scheduling (25+ years of flexible work history). Needs about 30% reactive work balanced against focused deep work time, ideally in stimulating environments like coffee shops. Thrives in small team collaborations (under 5 people) through quick video calls and thoughtful written communication rather than constant Slack interruptions. Values concrete next steps, listening, and forward momentum in team interactions. Requires schedule and location control to be productive, with minimal dependency on instant messaging tools.

## What Fills Them

Building connective tissue between valuable products and people who need them to work. Most energized watching team members figure things out in environments built for discovery, not by following prescribed playbooks. Drawn to translation problems where real people struggle and helpful products exist, but nobody has built the bridge yet — especially when stakes are high and consequences of failure matter. Gets energy from earning trust of skeptical-but-not-cynical frontline workers with 20 years of experience, then building systems that keep working after leaving the room. Thrives on customer escalations, difficult conversations, and one-on-one coaching.

## Disqualifiers

PE-backed companies (extraction timeline corrupts customer success function). Job descriptions leading with NRR ownership (inheriting someone else's scorecard). Companies over $75M funding or 150+ employees unless rebuilder situation, new product lines, or recent CS leadership turnover. Developer tools under 50 people without enterprise motion ($30K+ deals, multiple buying stakeholders, contract complexity). Environments where candor is managed or unwelcome. Pure IC roles without team-building path. Sub-$125K base salary (signals CS viewed as cost center). Polished interview processes with no team disagreement or evasive compensation discussions.

## Goals & Timeline

Director+ title minimum ($125K-$140K base) — signals organizational commitment to CS, not ego. Remote preferred but will relocate to NYC or SF for right opportunity, hybrid acceptable in NYC/SF/
