## Values

| Value | What does this word mean to us? |
|---|---|
| **1. Candor** | We tell clients what we actually think — about the market, the candidate, the role spec — even when it's uncomfortable. A search that starts with a bad brief costs everyone six months. We'd rather have the hard conversation on day one. |
| **2. Selectivity** | We run fewer searches than we could. Each partner carries three at a time, not eight. This is how we stay useful rather than busy. Selectivity is how we protect the work from becoming transactional. |
| **3. Curiosity** | The best CFO for Meridian is not the one with the most proximate résumé — it's the one whose pattern of decisions reveals the right instincts. That requires us to ask better questions than "walk me through your background." |
| **4. Discretion** | We handle material non-public information, career-defining decisions, and relationships between people who will work together for years. Nothing we learn in a search conversation goes anywhere it wasn't intended to go. Ever. |
| **5. Continuity** | We do not hand searches off to associates after the engagement is signed. The partner who closes the client is the partner on the phone at 11pm when the finalist gets cold feet. This matters more than we can explain in a pitch. |
| **6. Judgment** | We are being paid for an opinion, not a database query. If a client wanted a name list they could run a LinkedIn search. Our value is in the interpretation — who will actually thrive here, given what this company is about to go through. |
| **7. Speed with Integrity** | Urgency is real. Boards and CEOs do not have infinite patience and neither does the operating calendar. But we have watched searches collapse because someone cut a corner. We move fast and do not skip steps. |
| **8. Humility** | We are not always right. The candidate we passed on has sometimes gone on to be exceptional. We try to understand why. The search business rewards confidence, but the firms that last are the ones that stayed honest about what they don't know. |

## Beliefs

| Belief | What this means in practice |
|---|---|
| **The brief is the search.** | How a role is defined on day one determines what candidates surface, how they're evaluated, and whether the placement holds. A weak brief produces a weak slate. Most search failures are brief failures. |
| **Fit is two-directional.** | A candidate is not just being evaluated — they are evaluating. The best people have options. If we cannot articulate what makes this company and this moment genuinely compelling, we will lose the people we most want in the process. |
| **Reference checks are a skill, not a formality.** | Anyone can call three names from a résumé and get a glowing reference. Real reference work surfaces the operating conditions under which someone thrives — and the conditions under which they don't. That is what we're actually after. |
| **Chemistry is not culture fit.** | "Culture fit" is often a proxy for familiarity. We push clients to distinguish between the two. A candidate who makes a hiring committee comfortable and a candidate who will strengthen the organization are not always the same person. |
