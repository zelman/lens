## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Four independent AI models stress-tested the Lens market thesis across two prompt versions. The findings converge on a clear strategic resequencing: **lead with the role lens and stakeholder alignment, not candidate identity extraction.** The "observer-informed" academic claim is unanimously flagged as overstated. Defensibility is rated 3-4/10 on technology but improvable through process embedding and white-label adoption. The $10M ARR path is plausible in 4-6 years; $50M+ requires market expansion beyond retained exec search.

## CONVERGENT FINDINGS (all 3-4 models agree — treat as ground truth)

### 1. Stop leading with Oh/Wang/Mount

**Consensus:** The claim that a 45-minute AI conversation produces "observer-informed" data is a stretch. The AI has no independent behavioral history — it's categorizing what the candidate says about themselves. This is structured, conversational self-report, not observer-rated assessment.

**What to do:**
- Cite Oh/Wang/Mount as *inspiration for why extraction depth matters*, not as *validation of Lens's specific approach*
- Reframe as: "Higher-resolution structured self-report that enables scoring resumes and surveys can't"
- Add external signals in Phase 2-3 (peer input, reference data, prior manager feedback) to move toward genuine observer-grade validity
- Plan a validation study (Lens scores vs. supervisor ratings vs. performance outcomes) as a Phase 3 milestone

**New language:** "Psychometric assessments already exist — they're standardized self-report instruments. Coached conversational extraction produces richer, more structured signal because the AI probes, challenges, and draws out what surveys miss. The research suggests observer-informed inputs are more predictive; our roadmap includes adding those external signals."

### 2. Lead with role lens, not candidate lens

**Consensus:** The wedge into exec search is NOT "help me understand this candidate better." It's "help the five partners at this firm agree on what they're actually looking for — and produce a client-facing artifact that's obviously better than the Word doc they currently send."

**Why this works:**
- Solves an immediate, visible pain: late-stage "this candidate isn't the right kind of leader" surprise that kills searches
- Ties directly to revenue risk for the buyer (losing a retainer, failed search)
- Shows up before candidates enter the funnel — less friction, higher urgency
- Creates the natural bridge to candidate lenses once role lens is established

**Product reframe:** "The system that prevents expensive misalignment in executive search" — not "AI identity layer."

### 3. Live at the client boundary, not inside the recruiter's workflow

**Consensus:** If Lens lives inside the ATS (recruiter's internal workflow), it's a feature that gets absorbed. If it lives at the client-recruiter boundary — producing client-facing deliverables — it's a product.

**Implication:** The lens output must be designed as a client-facing artifact first. The role lens becomes the "search brief" that looks obviously better than what firms currently produce in Word/PowerPoint. Candidate lenses become the "shortlist comparison" that clients review alongside CVs.

### 4. Portable document is a feature, not a moat

**Consensus:** "We allow export" is trivially copyable. Torre, Plum, or Jack & Jill could add a download button in one sprint. Portability only becomes a moat if:
- The Lens schema becomes a de facto standard that other platforms integrate with
- The document accumulates networked data (endorsements, outcomes, recruiter notes) that make it costly to ignore

**What to do:** Stop counting portability in the moat stack. Use it in sales narrative ("you own your data") but build real defensibility through process embedding and outcome data.

### 5. Defensibility comes from process embedding, not technology

**Consensus:** 3-4/10 on technology defensibility across all models. The patent helps but is narrow and work-aroundable. Real moat sources:
- **White-label play:** Let early firms brand Lens output as their proprietary methodology → hard to rip out once they're marketing it to clients
- **Outcome data:** Track search outcomes (time-to-slate, offer acceptance, early attrition) → builds evidence that incumbents can't replicate without years of usage data
- **Process embedding:** If Lens defines how a firm does stakeholder alignment, switching cost is high regardless of whether the tech is copyable

### 6. SquarePeg's lesson: buyers pay for less work, not better judgment

**Consensus:** Recruiters believe their judgment IS the product. They don't pay for "better ontology of identity." They pay for:
- Less work (time savings)
- Better optics (more rigorous process to show clients)
- Deal-winning (differentiator in competitive pitches)

**Implication:** Every pitch must lead with one of these, not with "deeper understanding of who candidates really are."

### 7. Goldman validation is useful framing, not market proof

**Consensus:** Edie Hunt's input is excellent for segment focus and pricing framing. It is not a substitute for: "How many partners at Firm X will commit to paying $Y per search and actually run candidates through this for 6 months?"

**What to do:** Treat it as an anecdote in sales pitches. Prioritize live validation conversations (Beth Stewart, Birdsong) that can generate actual purchase intent signals.

### 8. The first customer is a 10-20 person retained boutique

**Consensus (strongest from Perplexity v2):** The first buyer is a sector-focused retained boutique (B2B SaaS, healthcare, PE-backed portfolio cos), 10-20 people, 20-60 C-level searches/year, $150-250K average fee. The founding partner:
- Uses Invenias or Clockwork plus spreadsheets
- Prides themselves on white-glove discovery
- Is under pressure from PE/board clients to show more "rigor"
- Has recently lost a search or had a mis-hire post-mortem

**Trigger event:** A high-profile mis-hire that leads to tense post-mortem, or a competitive pitch where the firm fears losing to a bigger firm touting proprietary psychometrics.

### 9. Per-search pricing, not per-seat SaaS

**Consensus:** $2-5K per search package (1 role lens + N candidate lenses + client-ready reporting). At $4K/search across 2,500 searches/year → $10M ARR. Evolve to firm-level subscriptions ($30-80K/year) as proof of value accumulates.

### 10. This is services-adjacent SaaS, not pure SaaS

**Consensus:** The $10M path requires founder-led, consultative sales. Deep workflow integration. Trust building with individual partners. This is NOT a self-serve PLG motion. Think "Bloomberg Terminal for exec search" (Gemini's framing) — professional-grade standard of practice, not a website.

## DIVERGENT OR UNIQUE FINDINGS (need live validation to resolve)

### Is the coaching methodology a real differentiator or a narrative wrapper?

- **Perplexity:** Skeptical — says conversation is just "fancy follow-ups" on self-disclosure
- **ChatGPT:** Neutral — says the end-to-end workflow + artifact + analytics could be a product even without the science claim
- **Gemini:** Most positive — frames the coaching methodology as "process IP" that compounds when embedded in firms

**Resolution needed:** Run 5+ tester sessions comparing Lens output quality to a simple GPT-4o interview (no coaching methodology). If there's no measurable difference in signal richness, the methodology claim is hollow. If there is, document it as evidence.

### Can incumbents ship this in one sprint?

- **Perplexity v2:** Yes, "conversational intake powered by OpenAI" is one sprint — but the end-to-end workflow is harder
- **ChatGPT:** The conversation is a feature; the client-facing discovery + alignment layer is a product
- **Gemini:** Incumbents move slower than expected; process IP + brand compound if embedded early

**Resolution needed:** Time is the answer. Ship fast, embed deep, build outcome data before incumbents notice the category.

### $50M+ outcome probability

- **Perplexity v1:** 4-5/10 overall
- **Perplexity v2:** 3-4/10 (requires expansion beyond exec search)
- **ChatGPT:** Didn't rate numerically but implied narrow
- **Gemini:** Highest implied confidence if pivots are executed

**Resolution needed:** Accept that $50M+ standalone is unlikely. The realistic outcomes are: (a) profitable narrow niche ($5-15M ARR), (b) acquisition by LinkedIn/Bullhorn/assessment platform once category is proven, or (c) expansion into in-house executive talent at PE funds and large enterprises.

## REVISED PRODUCT ROADMAP

### Phase 1 (0-12 months): Role lens + client alignment
- Nail the role lens intake: structured capture of stakeholder misalignment, trade-offs on mission/culture/leadership style
- Produce a client-facing "search brief" artifact in International Style that's obviously better than Word/PowerPoint
- Show time saved building briefs + fewer late-stage "wrong kind of leader" surprises
- 10-20 lighthouse firms, founder-led sales, white-label option

### Phase 2 (12-24 months): Candidate lens + shortlists
- Add candidate intake where firms are willing
- Frame as: "Lens produces a comparable, scorable view across all shortlisted candidates relative to the role lens"
- Begin adding external signals (references, peer input) to move toward observer-grade validity
- Surface simple analytics: values alignment, risk flags, energy-role fit

### Phase 3 (24-36 months): Outcomes + defensibility
- Track search outcomes: time-to-slate, time-to-fill, offer acceptance, early attrition
- Run validation study: Lens scores vs. supervisor ratings vs. performance outcomes
- Use outcome data to justify higher pricing and build data moat
- Expand to in-house executive talent teams at PE funds and large enterprises
