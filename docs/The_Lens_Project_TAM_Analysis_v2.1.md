# Executive Summary

The Lens Project is an AI-powered career identity discovery and job-fit platform that produces portable, machine-readable lens documents through coaching-depth discovery, then scores opportunities against those documents using signal matching rather than keyword matching. The core value proposition: the gap in hiring is not in filtering — it is in what is being filtered. No existing tool collects the behavioral signals, energy patterns, values-in-practice, and disqualifiers that predict whether someone will thrive in a specific role.

This revision expands the original TAM model to incorporate use cases identified through product development and stakeholder conversations:

1. **Active Job Seekers (PLG)** — original model, refined
2. **Passive Monitoring for Employed Professionals** — "tell me if I should pay attention"
3. **Coach-as-Channel (B2B2C)** — coaches license the tool as a deliverable for clients
4. **Freemium Lead Generation** — free lens creation → paid pipeline (coaching, daily briefings)
5. **VC / Recruiter Extension** — intelligence layer for institutional buyers
6. **Corporate / Outplacement** — enterprise buyers for employees in transition (SPECULATIVE — pending employer-side validation)

## Use Case 4: Freemium / Free Tool (Todd Model)

Freemium funnel: "Sign up to create a sample report" → conversion to paid tiers.

This is a go-to-market strategy rather than a distinct TAM segment, but it changes funnel math significantly.

Free tier attracts: broad awareness audience of operators who encounter the tool through referral, content marketing, or coach recommendation.

Estimated annual free signups (achievable with coach channel + content): ~5,000–15,000

Conversion to paid (estimated 3–8%): ~150–1,200 paid users

Paid tier options:
- Self-serve briefing subscription ($40–$60/month)

- Full-service tier ($300–$500/month) — includes human coaching sessions

**Freemium funnel ceiling: ~500–1,200 paid users across tiers**

The freemium model also serves as the lead generation engine for the coach channel and consultative tiers.

# 6. Strategic Implications by Use Case

## Free Tool (Todd Model) as GTM Engine

The freemium entry point serves three strategic functions:
1. **Lead generation** for paid tiers — the sample report demonstrates value
2. **Coach channel activation** — coaches recommend the free tool as onboarding for clients
3. **Data flywheel** — even free users contribute signal data (what they score, what they skip)

The free tier is not a revenue center. It is a distribution mechanism.

## Passive Monitoring as Retention Engine

Active seekers churn when they land a role (3–6 month average lifecycle). Passive monitoring users retain as long as the briefings remain relevant — potentially 12–36 months. This cohort stabilizes revenue and provides the recurring base that makes the business investable.

The product shift: active seekers need urgency and volume. Passive users need signal quality and low noise. The lens document serves both, but the briefing cadence and threshold tuning differ.

## Coach Channel as Quality Moat

Coaches solve the cold-start problem: a lens document authored through real coaching methodology is deeper than one authored via AI-only discovery. Better documents produce better scoring. Better scoring produces better retention. Coach-authored lenses become the quality benchmark that pure self-serve can't match — which justifies the premium tier.

Revenue sharing with coaches (10–20% of downstream subscription revenue from their clients) creates aligned incentives and lock-in.

# 7. Key Risks

**Retention in active seeker cohort:** Average job search is 3–6 months. If most users are active seekers, churn is structural. Passive monitoring and coach channel mitigate this.

**Coach adoption friction:** Coaches are not technologists. The authoring process must be seamless enough that coaches can facilitate it without training. If the tool requires technical skill, coach adoption stalls.

**Signal quality at scale:** The current system works because the lens document is deep (months of coaching produced it). If the free/self-serve tier produces shallow lenses, scoring quality degrades, and word-of-mouth turns negative.

**Competitive risk from incumbents:** LinkedIn, Indeed, and large job platforms could add "preference profiles." Their advantage is distribution. The Lens Project's advantage is depth — signal matching vs. keyword matching. The question is whether depth matters enough to sustain a niche against broad platforms with good-enough matching.

**Free tool indistinguishable from ChatGPT + resume:** A sophisticated user who uploads their materials to ChatGPT with a well-crafted prompt could get a comparable throughline analysis. The raw AI capability is not the moat. The moat is: (1) structured output format — machine-readable YAML that a scoring pipeline can parse, which ChatGPT does not produce; (2) methodology encoding — coaching frameworks that push past surface answers, not a raw chatbot; (3) pipeline integration — daily scoring the user cannot replicate without significant technical setup. If the free tier does not produce a visible "aha" moment that feels qualitatively different from a ChatGPT conversation, the freemium funnel breaks at the top and all downstream revenue scenarios degrade. This is the most important signal to watch in Experiment 1 (Lens Shock Test).

**Enterprise thesis unvalidated:** James Pratt's feedback (3/30/26) challenges the assumption that companies want hiring precision over volume. Scenarios F and G depend on employer-side adoption. Until validated with actual hiring leaders (Edie Hunt call April 7 is the first test), institutional revenue projections are speculative ceilings.

# Core Insight (Revised)

The Lens Project is supply-constrained but multi-channel.

Per-persona TAM for active seekers is narrow. But the same supply-side intelligence serves passive monitoring, coach-mediated discovery, institutional buyers, and corporate transition programs — each with different pricing, retention, and lifetime value characteristics.

The freemium entry point (Todd model) activates the funnel. The coach channel (James Pratt model) deepens quality. Passive monitoring (employed-but-open users) stabilizes revenue. Institutional buyers (VC, recruiter, outplacement) provide high-ARPU anchors.

Single-persona PLG ceiling: <$250k ARR
Multi-channel, multi-persona ceiling: $4M–$10M ARR
The difference is not the technology. It is the go-to-market architecture.
